The Skagit Alpine Club will offer two types of courses this year:

Basic Mountaineering Course- 8 week course This 8-week course provides an introduction to outdoor rock climbing, navigation, ice axe arrest, rope team travel, lead belay, and crevasse rescue. The course utilizes Mountaineering: Freedom of the Hills as foundational reading prior to experiential coursework. Each week includes readings, classroom practice, and an outing that will provide recurring practice with skills a mountaineer will need to travel safely in the alpine. Participants must show basic skills competence during weekday session prior to weekend outing.
Early Registration Rate (By February 1st)- $500
Registration (Feb 2nd-March 1st)- $550

Intro to Snow Travel & Ice Axe Arrest- 1 week course This course will address basic skills and safety using ice axes, basic avalanche awareness and snow travel practice. Open to all students, no previous experience with snow travel necessary. Participants must show basic skills competence during weekday session prior to weekend outing.
Cost- $200

Important Course Dates

  • Registration Opens: January 2nd
  • Information session: January 19th- Location coming soon
  • Registration Closes: March 1st
  • First Course starts: March 23rd

Fitness Levels
Each outing will be physically demanding and requires strong hiking fitness and endurance. We recommend that all students engage in several weeks of regular hiking experience with more than 1,000ft of elevation gain each hike prior to starting the course. Strength training experience is also recommended. Participants participating in the 8 week course should be able to carry 30lbs up the Pine & Cedar Trail in the Chuckanuts in under 1 hour and 30 minutes.

Course Fees
Course cost is still being determined.

All Course registrations include a one-year membership to the Skagit Alpine Club meetings, newsletters, and events.

COVID Notice
All courses are subject to change based on CDC guidelines. If courses are cancelled based on COVID restrictions, a full refund will be provided. CDC guidelines will be followed throughout courses, and students may be asked to leave without refund if not adhering to policies. Students will be asked to complete a COVID Waiver to acknowledge policies prior to course start.

Cancellation & Refund Policy
Payment required in full at time of registration. After registration, full refunds are available until two weeks prior to the start of the first course date. No refunds available after two weeks before first course date. For the safety of all, students experiencing any possible symptoms of COVID or exposed to anyone with COVID symptoms should report them immediately. Students will get a full refund or transfer to a later course if an isolated quarantine period is required.

If courses are cancelled based on COVID restrictions, a full refund will be provided. Courses that do not meet the minimum enrollment may be cancelled with full refund or offer transfers to other course dates.
